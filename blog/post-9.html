From Flood-Ravaged Farm to $1 Million Global Prize

Kenyan farmer Noah Nasiali lost everything in a flood. Then Facebook gave him $1 million.

July 16, 2026 — By Locust Harvest

In 2018, Kenyan farmer Noah Nasiali watched torrential rains wash away his entire farm — six greenhouses, six acres of crops, everything he had built over years of hard work. It was devastating. The flood didn't just take his crops; it took his livelihood, his savings, and his hope.

Noah had poured everything into his farm. He had built greenhouses, invested in irrigation, and cultivated relationships with buyers. Then the rains came — heavier than anyone had seen in years. The water swept through his land, destroying everything in its path. He was left with nothing but debt and a decision: give up or fight back.

The Turning Point

In the midst of his grief, Noah did something unexpected. He filmed a short 30-second video explaining that an upcoming training session for farmers had to be cancelled. He posted it on his personal Facebook page, not expecting much. That post got 1.2 million views. Farmers from across Kenya reached out offering help. Some even showed up physically to help him rebuild. The community rallied around him in a way he never imagined possible.

Then Facebook called. Noah was selected for their Community Leaders in Residence program, representing Africa and the Middle East. He was awarded $1 million to continue his work.

Today, his Afarmers AgriTech & Leadership Centre is a pan-African platform empowering smallholder farmers through digital tools, training, and innovation. What began as a flood-ravaged farm has become a symbol of resilience — proof that even in tragedy, purpose can grow.

"What was meant to destroy me became my greatest opportunity."

— Noah Nasiali
