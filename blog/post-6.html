From Street Hawker to Millionaire

After 21 years of selling bread on Lagos highways, Lanre's life transformed in hours — thanks to a viral video and the generosity of Nigerians.

July 11, 2026 — By Locust Harvest

For 21 years, Lanre walked the dangerous highways of Lagos, selling bread. He woke up before dawn, braved reckless traffic, endured harassment from motorists, and came home each day with barely enough to survive. His profit on each loaf? Just N100 (about 20 cents).

At 49 years old, he had nothing to show for two decades of hard work — except his dignity and a promise he had kept for 21 years.

The Promise

When his mother passed away, Lanre made a vow: he would never smoke or drink. For 21 years, through poverty, loneliness, and the daily grind of street hawking, he kept that promise.

The Turning Point

One day, a chance encounter changed everything. Bayuz TV, a TikTok philanthropist, met Lanre on the streets of Lagos. He listened to his story, filmed a video, and shared it with the world.

The video went viral — millions of views in hours.

The Profit

The outpouring of support was immediate and overwhelming:

  • Initial gift: ₦50,000 + medical assistance
  • Second meeting: Donations reached ₦4.45 million
  • Total donations: Over GH¢43,200 (approximately ₦6 million)
  • Monthly allowance: ₦50,000 promised
  • Children's school fees: Fully covered

Lanre broke down in tears. After 21 years of struggle, he was now a millionaire in hours.

"I notice this life ehh, if you dey help people you no go broke."

— Social media user

What's Next

The funds are being used to start his own bread-making business, transition from street hawking to entrepreneurship, secure his children's education, and build a stable future for his family.
